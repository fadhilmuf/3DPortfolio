In my thesis, I have focused on developing a hydrogen generator using PEM water electrolysis. To achieve this, I utilized a dry cell system, with SUS316 as the plate material and nylon mesh 300 as the membrane. As for the power source, I opted for solar panels to supply the required input power. To accomplish my goal, I designed and built the necessary tools, which comprised a 3-cell electrolysis setup. My objective was to create a hydrogen generation system that would be both efficient and cost-effective, while also being environmentally friendly. Through my research, I have been able to develop a system that meets all of these criteria and offers the potential to make a significant contribution to the renewable energy sector.
