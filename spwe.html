Water Electrolysis

In my research, I have focused on developing a hydrogen generator using PEM water electrolysis. To achieve this, I utilized a dry cell system, with SUS316 as the plate material and nylon mesh 300 as the membrane. As for the power source, I opted for solar panels to supply the required input power. To accomplish my goal, I designed and built the necessary tools, which comprised a 3-cell electrolysis setup. My objective was to create a hydrogen generation system that would be both efficient and cost-effective, while also being environmentally friendly. Through my research, I have been able to develop a system that meets all of these criteria and offers the potential to make a significant contribution to the renewable energy sector.

In my recent testing, I conducted an experiment between 2:00 pm and 2:15 pm under clear weather conditions, using a solar panel as the power source. The system was configured to supply an external current of 3 amperes. The purpose of the experiment was to measure the hydrogen and oxygen production rates using a PEM water electrolysis system with a dry cell setup. After analyzing the results, I found that the hydrogen production rate was approximately 2 grams, while the oxygen production rate was approximately 4 grams. These findings provide valuable insights into the efficiency of the electrolysis system and help to identify areas where improvements can be made. Overall, the experiment was a success, and I look forward to conducting further research to refine the system's performance and capabilities.
